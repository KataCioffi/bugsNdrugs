  • Causative pathogens
    • Strep GAS, S. aureus
    • 1st line therapy
      • prophylaxis: augmentin 4:1 ratio 10mg/kg TID or 7:1 ratio 22.5mg/kg BID x 3-5 days
      • active infection: augmentin 4:1 ratio 10mg/kg TID or 7:1 ratio 22.5mg/kg BID x 5-14 days, should be continued 1-2 days after symptoms have resolved or 14 days whichever comes first
    • Combination therapies:
      • cefuroxime 10-15 mg/kg BID, doxycycline 1-2 mg/kg BID, OR TMP-SMX 4-6 mg/kg
      • PLUS
      • Metrondiazole 10 mg/kg TID or clindamycin 10 mg/kg TID x 3-5 days (prophylaxis) 5-14 days (active infection; should be continued 1-2 days after symptoms have resolved)

  • Causative pathogens
    • Borrelia Burgdorferi
    • Early stage:
      • doxycycline 2 mg/kg/dose BID x 14-21 days, less than 8 yrs= amoxicillin 20-50 mg/kg/day divided TID x 14-21 days
    • late stage:
      • persistent arthritis/carditis/neuro disease; Penicillin G or ceftriaxone PIV (refer for in patient treatment)
    • prophylaxis therapy:
      • If in endemic area AND tick removed >72 hours AND engorged with blood AND tick was ID'd as ixodes tick use PEP:less than 45kg= 4.4mg/kg x 1 dose >45kg= 200 mg

  • Causative pathogens
    • sarcoptes (aka “itch mite”)

      • Bath and dry child; apply permethrin 5% x1, leave on for 8-12 hours, wash off. Repeat in one week
    • Pruritis:
      • topical hydrocortisone x2/day, cetirizine 2.5-5 mg/day

  • Causative pathogens
    • (head) pediculosis capitis, (body) pediculosis humanes (pubic) phthirus pubis
    • Head:
      • Permethrin; soak child’s head and apply permethrin, let sit for 10 minutes. Comb out ova/nits afterward. Repeat in 1 week.
    • Body/Pubic:
      • Permethrin cream 1%; Clean area, apply cream, let sit for 10 minutes and wash off. Remove nits/ova after. Repeat 7-10 days
