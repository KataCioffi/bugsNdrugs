Cardiac

  • Causative pathogens
    • S.aureaus, S. pneumoniae, Strepococcus, Staphylococci, Enterococcus
    • 1st line therapy
      • Gentamicin + PCN 3 mg/kg/day TID IV/IM x 406 weeks
      • Gentamicin + Ceftriaxone
    • 2nd line therapy
      • Nafcillin + Gentamicin 4-6 weeks
      • Oxacillin + Gentamicin 4-6 weeks
    • Alternative therapies
      • Vancomycin + Gentamicin x 4-6 weeks

  • Causative pathogens
    • Secondary to GABHS Pharyngitis
    • 1st line therapy
      • Penicillin V: less than 27 kg: 250mg/kg/day divided BID/TID x 10 days; if greater than 27 kg:500 mg/kg/day divided BID/TID x 10 days
      • Amoxicillin 50 mg/kg/day x 10 days
      • Pencillin G: less than 27 kg: 600,000 unit IM x1 dose; if greater than 27 kg: 1.2 million units IM x 1 dose
    • 2nd line line therapy; allergy to PCNs
      • Cephalaxin 40 mg/kg/day BID x 10 days
      • Cefuroxime 10 mg/kg/day BID x 10 days
      • Cefpodoxime 5 mg/kg/dose BID x 5-10 days
      • Cefdinir 7 mg/kg/dose BID x 5-10 days or 14 mg/kg/dose QD x10 days
      • Azithromycin 12 mg/kg/dose BID x 5 days
      • Clarithromycin 7.5 mg/kg/dose BID x 10 days
      • Clindamycin 7 mg/kg/dose TID x 10 days
    • Prophylaxis Guidelines
      • RF without carditis: Pen G IM Q 28 days- continue for 5 years or until age 21 yrs; whichever is longer
      • RF with carditis, but no valvular disease: Pen G IM Q 28 days- continue for 10 years or until age 21 yrs; whichever is longer
      • RF with carditis & valvular disease: Pen G IM Q 28 days- continue 10 years or until age 40 yrs; whichever is longer
