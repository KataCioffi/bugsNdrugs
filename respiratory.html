Respiratory

  • Causative pathogens
    • Neonates: GBS, enterococcos, L. monocytogenes, E. coli, U.Urealyticum
    • 1-3 month: S. pneumoniae, Chlamydia, B.Pertussis, H. Influenzae, S. aureas, Ureaplasma
    • 4 month - 4 years: S. pneumoniae, H. Influenzae, S. aureas
    • All ages: S. pneumoniae, M. pneumoniae, H. Influenzae, S. aureas
    • Neonates:
      • Should be hospitalized for treatment
      • PIV Ampicillin + Gentamycin
      • If C. trichomatis is suspected + erythromycin x10 days
    • 3 month- 5 yrs:
      • Amoxicillin 90mg/kg/day x 7-10 days +/- Azithromycin 10 mg/kg day 1, then 5 mg/kg days 2-5
    • 5 yrs +:
      • Amoxicillin 90mg/kg/day x 7-10 days
      • Azithromycin 10 mg/kg day 1, then 5 mg/kg days 2-5
        •
    • Allergy to PCNs:
      • Levofloxacin 8-10 mg/kg/day divided BID x 5 days (less than 5 yrs)
      • Levofloxacin 8-10 mg/kg/day QD x 5 days (6 yrs+)
      • Clindamycin 10-13 mg/kg/dose x TID x 5 days

  • Causative pathogens
    • B. pertussis
    • 1st line therapy
      • Azithromycin 10 mg/kg/day x 1 day, then 5 mg/kg/day x 4 days; if less than 1 mo, 10 mg/kg/day x 5 days
      • Clarithromycin 14 mg/kg/day divided BID x 7 days
      • Erythromycin 40-50 mg/kg/day divided QID x 14 days, should not be used in kiddos less than 1 mo
    • Chemprophylaxis
      • All family members or close contact should be given:
      • Erythromycin 40-50 mg/kg/day divided QID x 14 days
    • Alternative therapies
      • Bactrim (TMP-SMX) 8 (TMP)/40(SMX) mg/kg/day divided BID x 14 days
        •

  • Causative pathogens
    • M. tuberculosis
    • 1st line therapy
      • Isoniazid (H) 10 mg/kg/day
      • Rifampin (R) 15 mg/kg/day
      • Pyrazinamide (Z) 35 mg/kg/day
      • Ethambutol (E) 20 mg/kg/day
    • Treatment regimines
      • Areas with low levels of HIV/isoniazid resistance: 3 drug regimine HRZ x 2 months, then 2 drug regimine HR x 4 months
      • Areas with high levels of HIV/isoniazid resistance: 4 drug regimine HRZE x 2 months, then 2 drug regimine HR x 4 months

  • Causative pathogens
    • Parainfluenza virus
    • 1st line therapy
      • Mild-Moderate: Dexamethasone 0.6mg/kg x1
      • Severe: Dexamethasone 0.6mg/kg x1 + nebulized 2.25% racemic-epinephrine 0.05ml/kg diluted with sterile saline
    • Treatment regimines
      • adequate hydration, antipyretics, and cool mist

  • Causative pathogens
    • C. diphtheriae, C. ulcerans
  • 1st line therapy
    • erythromycin 40-50 mg/kg/day divided QID x 14 days
  • Alternative Treatment
    • pen G + Pen VK 100,000-150,000 u/kg/250 mg Q4-6 hour PIV/IM x 14 days
  • Follow up/preventative care
    • reculture after treatment, treat household members
