GI

  • Causative pathogens
    • shingella, salmonella, yersinia, campylobacter jejuni, E. coli
    • 1st line therapy
      • shingella: ciprofloxin 30 mg/kg/day BID x 3-7 days
      • salmonella: azithromycin 10 mg/kg/day x 3-7 days, up to 14 days
      • yersinia: trimethoprim-sulfamethaxazole 8 mg/kg/day BID x 5 days,
      • campylobacter jenjuni: erythromycin 30-50 mg/kg/day QID x 5 days
      • e. coli: azithromycin 20 mg/kg/day x 3 days
    • 2nd line therapy
      • shingella: azithromycin 6-20 mg/kg/day x 3-7 days
      • salmonella: ceftriaxone 50-100 mg IM (severe cases)
      • yersinia: ciprofloxin 3mg/kg/day BID x 3-7 days
      • campylobacter jenjuni: azithromycin 10 mg/kg/day x 3-5 days
    • Alternative therapies
      • shingella: ceftriaxone 50-100 mg IM (severe cases)

  • Causative pathogens
    • Giardia
    • 1st line therapy
      • Giardia: less than 3 years= Flagyl: 15 mg/kg TID x 5-7 days
      • Giardia: greater than 3 years= tinidazole: 50 mg/kg x1
    • 2nd line therapy
      • Giardia: nitazoxanide 100-200 m BID x 3 days, 500 mg BID if greater than 12 years old

  • Causative pathogens
    • H. pylori
    • 1st line therapy
      • H. Pylori: PPI+ amoxicillin + clarithromycin x 14 days
    • 2nd line therapy
      • H. Pylori: PPI+ amoxicillin x 5 days then PPI+ clarithromycin + Metrondiazole x 5 days

  • Causative pathogens
    • Enterobius Vermicularis
    • 1st line therapy
      • Pyrantel pamoate 11 mg/kg x 1 dose
      • Albendazole 400 mg x 1 dose; repeat in 2 weeks
    • Prevention
      • Keep fingernails short
      • Bathing removes eggs from skin, reducing pruritis
      • Hand Hygiene
      • Treat household if + similar symptoms
